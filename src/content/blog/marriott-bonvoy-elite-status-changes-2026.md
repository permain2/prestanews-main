Marriott has announced significant changes to its Bonvoy loyalty program effective January 2026. These updates affect elite tier qualification requirements and member benefits across all status levels. Here's what every Marriott loyalist needs to know.

## Summary of Marriott Bonvoy 2026 Changes

The changes impact three key areas: qualification requirements, elite benefits, and points earning rates.

### New Elite Night Requirements

| Status Level | 2025 Requirement | 2026 Requirement |
|-------------|------------------|------------------|
| Silver Elite | 10 nights | 10 nights (unchanged) |
| Gold Elite | 25 nights | 25 nights (unchanged) |
| Platinum Elite | 50 nights | 50 nights (unchanged) |
| Titanium Elite | 75 nights | 80 nights |
| Ambassador Elite | 100 nights + $23K | 100 nights + $25K |

The most significant changes affect the top two tiers, making Titanium and Ambassador status slightly harder to achieve.

## What's Changing for Elite Members?

### Improved Suite Upgrade Confirmations

Marriott is enhancing the suite upgrade program:

- **Titanium and Ambassador members** will now receive upgrade confirmations up to 5 days before arrival (previously 2 days)
- **Suite Night Awards (SNAs)** will process 7 days before check-in
- **New rollover policy**: Unused SNAs will now roll over for 6 months

This is welcome news for members who plan trips around upgrades.

### Enhanced Late Checkout

- **Gold Elite**: Guaranteed 2 PM checkout (up from best available)
- **Platinum Elite**: Guaranteed 4 PM checkout
- **Titanium/Ambassador**: Guaranteed 6 PM checkout when available

### Changes to Breakfast Benefits

Platinum and above members will see adjustments:

- **Full breakfast** confirmed at participating properties
- **$30 credit** at properties without complimentary breakfast
- **Clear policy documentation** for international properties

## Who Wins and Who Loses?

### Winners

**Platinum Elite members** gain the most from these changes. Enhanced breakfast benefits and guaranteed late checkout significantly improve the value proposition.

**Frequent upgraders** will appreciate the earlier SNA processing. Planning trips becomes easier when you know about upgrades sooner.

### Losers

**Road warriors** chasing Titanium status face 5 additional nights. For business travelers on the margin, this could push them back to Platinum.

**High spenders** targeting Ambassador see a $2,000 increase in the spend requirement. Combined with additional nights, Ambassador becomes more exclusive.

## Marriott's Rationale

According to Marriott's announcement, the changes aim to:

1. **Reward true loyalists** with better benefits
2. **Reduce elite crowding** at top tiers
3. **Improve upgrade availability** for highest-tier members
4. **Align with industry standards** post-pandemic

The company emphasizes that most members won't be affected by the night requirement increases.

How do Marriott's 2026 requirements compare?

### IHG One Rewards

Hyatt Globalist requires 60 nights—still the easiest top-tier status among major chains.
