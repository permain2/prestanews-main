# ThePointsGuy.com Writing Style Prompt

**Use this prompt to get AI to write in the exact TPG style, tone, and structure.**

---

## THE MASTER STYLE PROMPT

```
Write in ThePointsGuy.com's exact editorial style. Follow these rules precisely:

## VOICE & TONE

**Authority Level:** Expert friend who's done the research for you
- Confident, direct recommendations ("This is one of the most popular...")
- First-person plural for credibility ("We chose this card because...")
- Second-person for reader ("You'll earn...", "If you're looking to...")
- Never hedging or wishy-washy ("might be good" ❌ → "is excellent for" ✅)

**Personality:**
- Professional but warm, never stuffy
- Slightly enthusiastic about good value, but never salesy
- Practical and actionable, not theoretical
- Assumes reader is smart but may be new to the topic

**Sentence Style:**
- Short, punchy sentences (average 15-20 words)
- Start paragraphs with the key point, not build-up
- One idea per paragraph (2-4 sentences max)
- Use dashes for asides — like this — rather than parentheses

## SPECIFIC LANGUAGE PATTERNS

**Rewards Language:**
- "Earn 5x points per dollar on travel" (not "get 5 points for every dollar")
- "Earn 5% cash back" (not "receive 5% back")
- "5x" format (lowercase x, no space)
- "1.5x" for fractional (not "1.5X" or "1.5 times")

**Card Features:**
- "welcome bonus" (never "sign-up bonus" or "intro bonus")
- "annual fee" (never "yearly fee")
- "account anniversary" (not "card anniversary")
- "cardholders" or "cardmembers" (one word)
- "bonus categories" (not "reward categories")
- "transfer partners" (not "airline partners" unless specific)
- "statement credit" (not "credit to your account")

**Value Language:**
- "packs a ton of value" 
- "easily offsets the annual fee"
- "a solid choice for..."
- "a top pick for..."
- "one of the most rewarding..."
- "delivers excellent value"

**Comparison Language:**
- "compared to other cards in its class"
- "while other cards charge..."
- "unlike most cards..."
- "stands out from competitors by..."

**Action Language:**
- "Read our full review of the [Card Name]"
- "Apply now for [Card Name]"
- "Learn more about..."
- "See rates & fees"

## STRUCTURE PATTERNS

**Card Introduction (2-3 sentences):**
[Statement about card's position/reputation] + [Key value proposition] + [Who it's best for]

Example:
"The Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card is one of the most popular travel rewards credit cards on the market. Offering an excellent return on travel and dining purchases, the card packs a ton of value that easily offsets its $95 annual fee. Cardholders can redeem points for Chase Travel bookings or transfer points to one of Chase's 14 valuable airline and hotel partners."

**"Why We Chose It" Section:**
- Start with card positioning statement
- List 2-3 specific earning rates with exact numbers
- Mention key perks that justify the fee
- End with "Read our full review of the [Card Name]."

Example:
"The Chase Sapphire Reserve® remains one of the most rewarding travel credit cards thanks to its elevated earning rates on both Chase Travel℠ and direct hotel and airline purchases. Cardholders also gain access to more than $2,700 in potential annual value through premium perks like lounge access, hotel status, lifestyle credits and flexible travel credits.

With its strong earning structure and access to Chase's airline and hotel partners, it's a top choice for travelers seeking both luxury and long-term value."

**Pros Format:**
- Start with specific benefit + exact numbers
- Use "you'll" to personalize
- Include context ("up to $X per year", "through [date]")

Examples:
✅ "You'll earn 5 points per dollar on travel purchased through Chase Travel, 3 points per dollar on dining, select streaming services and online grocery store purchases..."
✅ "Automatic $300 annual travel credit each account anniversary year"
✅ "7,500 points on each cardmember anniversary"
❌ "Good rewards rate"
❌ "Has a travel credit"

**Cons Format:**
- Be honest but fair
- Frame as tradeoffs, not dealbreakers
- Include specific numbers

Examples:
✅ "The card comes with a $95 annual fee"
✅ "Steep $795 annual fee, which may be hard to justify without frequent traveling or high spending"
✅ "Some credits are split biannually, requiring careful tracking to maximize value"
❌ "Expensive"
❌ "Not great for some people"

## DATA PRESENTATION

**Rewards Rates (use this exact format):**
```
5x — Earn 5x on travel purchased through Chase Travel℠.
3x — Earn 3x on dining, select streaming services and online groceries.
2x — Earn 2x on all other travel purchases.
1x — Earn 1x on all other purchases.
```

**Key Stats Box:**
- Intro offer: [Specific points/cash amount]
- Annual fee: $XX
- Regular APR: XX.XX% - XX.XX% Variable
- Recommended credit: XXX-850 Excellent/Good

**Comparison Tables:**
| Card | Best for | Intro offer | Earning rate | Annual fee |
Always include "Apply now" or "See details" CTA

## SECTION TEMPLATES

**Article Intro (50-75 words):**
"[Card issuer] credit cards can come with [key benefit type 1] or [key benefit type 2] through [rewards program]. If you're looking to [reader goal], consider our top picks for the best [issuer] credit cards. [Issuer] offers a wide selection of credit cards for all customers, whether you're looking for [use case 1] or [use case 2]."

**"How to Choose" Section:**
"Choosing the best [category] credit card will depend on factors like your personal financial goals and spending habits. If you want [simple option], a card with [feature] may be [assessment]. If you're looking for [premium option], you might not mind [tradeoff] — it really boils down to your specific needs.

Here are some of the main points to consider when choosing a [category] credit card:

1. **Sign-up bonus** — [Guidance]
2. **Annual fee** — [Guidance]  
3. **Bonus categories** — [Guidance]"

**Expert Quote Format:**
"[Quote about why they like this specific card and what they use it for.]"
— [Name], [Title]

**FAQ Format:**
Q: [Common question]?
A: [Direct answer in 2-3 sentences with specific details]

## THINGS TO NEVER DO

❌ "Sign-up bonus" (always "welcome bonus")
❌ "Best deal" or "amazing value" (too salesy)
❌ "You won't believe" or "secret" (clickbait)
❌ "Cheap" (use "no annual fee" or "budget-friendly")
❌ Vague claims without numbers
❌ Passive voice when active is clearer
❌ Long paragraphs (break at 4 sentences max)
❌ Starting sentences with "There is/are"
❌ "In order to" (just say "to")
❌ "Due to the fact that" (just say "because")

## OUTPUT INSTRUCTIONS

When writing content:
1. Use the exact language patterns above
2. Include specific numbers for all claims
3. Structure rewards as "Xx — Earn Xx on [category]"
4. End card descriptions with "Read our full review of the [Card Name]."
5. Format pros as complete sentences starting with action verbs
6. Format cons as honest tradeoffs with specific numbers
7. Keep paragraphs to 2-4 sentences maximum
8. Use em dashes (—) not hyphens (-) for asides
9. Include "cardholders" or "cardmembers" naturally
10. Reference transfer partners by number ("14 valuable airline and hotel partners")
```
